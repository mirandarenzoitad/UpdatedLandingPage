K DRAMA

THE GLORY

     "The Glory" is a Korean drama that was released in 2021. The drama tells the story of a group of people who work at an IT company called T&T. The main character is Park Tae Suk, a team leader at T&T who is known for his quick thinking and sharp mind. He is tasked with leading his team to develop a new mobile game that will revolutionize the industry.Along the way, Park Tae Suk faces challenges from within the company, as well as from competitors who are trying to steal their ideas. He also develops a romantic relationship with Yoon Yoo Jin, a fellow T&T employee who is a talented designer. The drama explores themes such as corporate politics, workplace relationships, and the pursuit of success. It features a talented cast including Cha Tae Hyun, Lee Seung Gi, Moon Chae Won, and more. Overall, "The Glory" received positive reviews from viewers and critics alike for its engaging storyline, well-developed characters, and excellent performances by the cast. "The Glory" is a compelling drama that explores the challenges and rewards of working in the tech industry, while also delving into the personal lives of its characters.

LA CASA DE PAPEL

     "La Casa De Papel" is a Spanish-language television series, also known as "Money Heist" in English. The series was created by Álex Pina and produced by the Spanish television network Antena 3. It premiered in Spain in May 2017 and later became available on Netflix, where it gained a global audience. The plot of the series centers around a group of eight robbers, each with a unique skill set, who plan and execute a meticulously planned heist on the Royal Mint of Spain. The robbers take hostages and print their own money as they try to escape with a fortune. The series has been praised for its complex characters, thrilling plot, and innovative storytelling techniques. It has also been recognized for its exploration of political and social issues, such as economic inequality and corruption. "La Casa De Papel" has become one of the most popular non-English language series on Netflix and has been renewed for several seasons. AMERICAN SIT COM

BROOKLYN 99

     "Brooklyn Nine-Nine" is an American television series that aired from 2013 to 2021. The show was created by Dan Goor and Michael Schur and follows the lives of the detectives at the fictional 99th precinct of the NYPD in Brooklyn, New York. The series features a talented ensemble cast, including Andy Samberg as Detective Jake Peralta, Terry Crews as Sergeant Terry Jeffords, Stephanie Beatriz as Detective Rosa Diaz, and Andre Braugher as Captain Raymond Holt. The show is known for its blend of comedy and crime drama, and for its depiction of diverse characters and social issues. Throughout its eight seasons, "Brooklyn Nine-Nine" has received critical acclaim and has won numerous awards, including two Golden Globe Awards for Best Television Series – Musical or Comedy. It has also been praised for its diverse cast and storylines that address social issues such as police brutality, discrimination, and LGBTQ+ representation.

SCIENCE-FICTION

THE INTERSTELLAR

     "Interstellar" is a science fiction film directed by Christopher Nolan, released in 2014. The movie stars Matthew McConaughey, Anne Hathaway, and Jessica Chastain in lead roles, and explores the concept of interstellar travel and the possibility of human life in other galaxies. The movie is set in the near future, where humanity is struggling to survive due to the depletion of Earth's resources. McConaughey plays Cooper, a former pilot and astronaut who is recruited by a team of scientists to embark on a mission to find a new habitable planet for human life. Hathaway plays Dr. Brand, a scientist who is leading the mission, and Chastain plays Cooper's daughter, who remains on Earth while her father is on the mission. The film explores various themes such as time, love, and the nature of humanity. It features stunning visual effects and a complex, mind-bending storyline that challenges the audience's perception of reality. Interstellar was well-received by both audiences and critics, praised for its storytelling, visual effects, and performances. It was also nominated for several Academy Awards, including Best Original Score, Best Visual Effects, and Best Sound Mixing.




MARVEL CINEMATIC UNIVERSE

MARVEL MOVIES

     The Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) is a film franchise and shared universe created by Marvel Studios and owned by The Walt Disney Company. It began with the release of "Iron Man" in 2008 and has since expanded to include over 25 films, several TV shows, and comic book adaptations. The MCU is based on characters from Marvel Comics and features a diverse range of superheroes and villains, including Iron Man, Captain America, Thor, Hulk, Black Widow, Hawkeye, Spider-Man, Doctor Strange, and the Guardians of the Galaxy. These characters are brought together through a series of interconnected storylines that often lead to crossover events, such as "The Avengers" and "Avengers: Endgame." The films in the MCU are known for their action-packed sequences, humor, and exploration of complex themes such as identity, morality, and power. In addition to the films, the MCU has expanded to include several TV shows, including "Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.," "Daredevil," and "WandaVision," which also connect to the overarching narrative of the franchise. The success of the MCU has made it one of the highest-grossing film franchises of all time, with several of its films ranking among the top 10 highest-grossing films of all time. It has also received critical acclaim and numerous awards, including several Academy Awards for its technical achievements. The MCU has become a cultural phenomenon and has established itself as a major force in the entertainment industry.

FAST AND THE FURIOUS MOVIE SERIES

     The Fast and the Furious series is a long-running action film franchise that began with the release of "The Fast and the Furious" in 2001. The films follow a group of street racers and their various adventures involving high-speed car chases, heists, and international espionage. The franchise features a diverse cast of characters, including Dominic Toretto (Vin Diesel), Brian O'Conner (Paul Walker), Letty Ortiz (Michelle Rodriguez), and Roman Pearce (Tyrese Gibson), among others. The films are known for their fast-paced action, thrilling car stunts, and high-tech gadgets. The series has evolved over time, with later films introducing new characters and storylines that have expanded the franchise's scope beyond its original street-racing roots. The films have also been notable for their inclusion of strong female characters and diverse casting. The franchise has been commercially successful, with several of its films ranking among the highest-grossing films of all time. However, it has also faced criticism for its over-the-top action sequences and unrealistic portrayal of car culture. Despite the tragic death of Paul Walker in 2013, the series has continued with new installments, including "The Fate of the Furious" and "F9," which have continued to draw large audiences and maintain the franchise's popularity.
